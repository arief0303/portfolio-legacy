Prototype

Paper Prototype

Sprint 0

Before starting development of this portfolio, i need to conceptualize the style and how the interface should look like. My direction is to make a clean minimalist and presentable looking site with an intuitive interface.

The 1st Iteration

I did this first iteration in the early stage of the development of this site. This iteration is more like a rough estimation for the foundation of this site.


The 2nd Iteration

In the second iteration, i aim to refine and simplify the interface to give a more presentable outlook of the site. I also added details on how would the colour schemes be and other features.


The 3rd Iteration

In the third iteration i put further details from the previous iteration as i applied the colour scheme straight unto the prototype to further visualize the would be end product, and to put more context to describe the reasoning behind each design decisions.


Feedback

I have learnt that by making a paper prototype is the fastest way to start realizing an abstract idea although it requires multiple iterations to achieve the most desired result.



Storyboard

Sprint 1

A storyboard was originally used for the film industry. In designing user experience it is used as a tool to visualize an idea or a concept, to describe the context use of a product, and to show the emotional appeal of the product.

In this exercise i have made a visually strong yet simple and easily-understandable storyboard all drawn straight digitally.

In this storyboard it depicts on how would a reaction of a target audience of the client be when they first discover her song on a music streaming platform that is also complimented with a strong visual album cover. All of the sudden the listener drowns in the melody of the song and got hooked.

Feedback

As feedback from Jo-An the storyboard looks nice and understandable. I now know that a storyboard can be a useful tool for UX design as it enable me to visually portray the scenario easily.
